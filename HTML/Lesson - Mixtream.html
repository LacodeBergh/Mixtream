The Drums

Writing drums can be somewhat daunting if you are a novice musician. Most people tend to want to write the most complicated rhythms imaginable with little consinderation for the other instruments in the composition. The above drum groove illustrates the ability to take a simple 4/4 rock groove and displace the kick in the 4th bar by a by 16th note (Semiquaver) in order to give the section and odd-time feel. Remember to only play the crash at the start of every 4th bar or 8th bar, depending on the tempo and\or style of the song. In this case we will apply the 8 bar rule.

The Bass Guitar

The bass guitar. An instrument that is easy to learn, yet, tough to master. I used the key of C and played the mode known as the harmonic minor, or The Egyptian Scale. This mode has been used to write many songs, especially within the Metal genre, as the mode tends to have rather dark medlodic content. This bass line can also be used in a funk fusion song, if you up the tempo to 120bpm. However, for the sake of this lesson, spend some time recording this line at the tempo of 70bpm. The progression is as follows:

Some Recording Tips

High-pass filter on each channel in the mix at a minimum of 30hz. This will prevent the instruments from introducing excessive low-end into the mix that could create a muddy tone.
Send effects through a bus. Sending effects to a bus will allow channels to be effected without altering the sound of the instrument altogether, but instead subtly embelish upon the overall sound.
The microphone on the strainer must be at least 8-10dB lower than the Snare top microphone. The strainer is only meant to add bite to the overal tone of the snare and should not be very noticeable.
Mixing Bass Record two lines of bass, place high pass filter up to 210Hz on one channel and a low-pass filter down to 190Hz. This will cancel out 200Hz which will allow for the Kick drum to cut through the mix.
