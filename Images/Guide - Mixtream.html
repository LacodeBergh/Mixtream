Gear Guide

The following guide will help you decide which equipment you would need to buy to get the best out of this course, as well as affordable alternatives. This will be regularly updated if and when new gear is made available. Have fun browsing!

Equipment

Description
Computer/Mac An Apple Imac, Macbook, or Mac mini is highly recommended as a start to get the best out of this lesson series. This is due to the fact that Apple owns a DAW known as Logic, which is one of the most affordable and user-friendly pieces of software on the market, plus the resale value on Apple computers is great and you are able to get a secondhand imac for as little as R4000-00 and it is guaranteed to work beautifully. PC is also pretty good, but if you are limited in funding, software will be hard to come by as logic is Mac-only. If you are looking for something specific, however, feel free to keep reading below as we make suggestions on a number of DAWs to help you decide which may be best suited for you.
DAW (Digital Audio Workstation) DAW stands for "Digital Audio workstation" and it is the way in which you will be able to record your music, mix it, and play it back, you will need some hardware to make this work, but for now, let's look at the various platforms available on on the market. Logic - as discussed previously - is one of the most affordable user-friendly DAWs on the market, which is a great piece of software to get started on. Pro Tools offers a monthly subscription on their software with over 60 plugins, the downside being that you never own the software. Reaper is not free, but they do offer a 60-day trial and it costs $60 thereafter.
Cables You have the hardware, now all you need to do is connect them to each other. USB/Firewire cables will be necessay to connect your interface and various other devices such as outboard compressors, EQs, etc. XLR and jack cables for recording audio as well MIDI cables to record with electronic instruments such as synths and keyboards./td>
Monitors The website talkinmusic.com has a list on the 5 Best Low Budget Studio Monitors - Talkin Music. Feel free to browse it.
Storage Storage facilities such as External hard disk drives and CDs are one of the most important components of recording music, as it allows you to store your files. Be sure to make back-ups of your work!
